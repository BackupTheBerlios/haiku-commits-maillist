On Sun, Mar 16, 2008 at 6:24 PM, bga at BerliOS <bga at mail.berlios.de> wrote:
>
>  - Changed default address for failed DHCP requests from 192.168.0.x to
>   169.254.0.x, as this is the IANA reserved address for default
>   configuration.

This reminds me, I just watched a Google Tech Talk on Bonjour, aka
Zeroconf, and it seems like a technology that would be useful for
Haiku. The 169.254.* range is the default for Bonjour. Apparently the
Apple code for it is open source with a friendly license (Apache v2)
and they have the platform-specific code nicely contained. So porting
shouldn't be too hard, especially with our decent POSIX layer. Maybe
this would make a good idea for the Summer of Code, though it
certainly isn't critical or even necessary for the alpha release.

But it could be an interesting project and could make for some neat
demos of Haiku machines cooperating without configuration.

For those curious, here is the Google Tech Talk (definitely worth watching):

http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-7398680103951126462

Here is the Apple developer page for it:

http://developer.apple.com/networking/bonjour/index.html
