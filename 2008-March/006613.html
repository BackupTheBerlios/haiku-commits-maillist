2008/3/6, Jonas Sundström <jonas at kirilla.com>:
> "Axel Dörfler" <axeld at pinc-software.de> wrote:

> One might consider this one exception harmless.
>  I just hope it won't spread. There must be reasons
>  non-interactive text isn't selectable in the major GUIs,
>  browser content aside.

I can't see why it shouldn't spread.
Selecting any text is always useful.
Let's see a common problem I just encountered on windows:
I have to download the drivers for my graphics card (at work). Since I
dont' know which card this computer has, I open the screen properties.
There it says I have a Nvidia Quadro NVS 280 PCI.
Since I can't select this text, I'm forced to remember this when I go
to the nvidia site.
If I could select the text, I could just paste it in my browser, add
"driver" to it and do a google search.
I really can't see any reason against being able to select any text. I
would even make the BAlert text selectable.
