I believe Stippi's point was that you're not the copyright holder in this 
case, and therefore aren't allowed to change the copyright header at all 
(the license is pretty explicit in that respect). You can add your own 
header, though. I think you're always allowed to do that (regardless of how 
substantial your change was); you might just not be able to defend you 
copyright claim in court, if it came to that. So unless you just do some 
coding style cleanup or very small changes, it won't harm to add a header, 
I guess. Otherwise I'd simply leave the existing header alone.
