Urias McCullough wrote:
> IMHO, of course, I think it's better in the end to keep it commented out.
>
> I have seen quite a few newbies make the mistake of renaming
> UserBuildConfig.sample to UserBuildConfig - without commenting out
> anything... and thus, as BGA noted, it ends up becoming a template.
>
> Whenever I setup a new build environment, I nearly always make a copy
> of UserBuildConfig.sample and then edit it.
>
> Furthermore, these same newbies usually neglect to use jam -q - making
> it difficult to figure out what actually went wrong without
> hand-holding. I believe it yields a somewhat frustrating first-time
> building experience... not that it's anyone's fault but their own
> (RTFM and all).
>
>   
I *very* much agree here. This is how I started too and I think this is 
how most unix sample files do that in addition.
If you have no clue of what you can configure, you will start in copying 
over the sample file and then investigate, which option you might want 
to use.

To be sure, which option you can safely disable (or even should disable 
as it is broken currently or for some other reason), it takes you much 
more time and experience with the build system.
