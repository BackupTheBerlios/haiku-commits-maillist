2007/11/5, Bruno Albuquerque <bga at bug-br.org.br>:

> This reminds me: One thing that still makes Haiku not look as polished
> as it could be is the fact that there is flicker everywhere when things
>   get updated on screen. Examples are the Media Player slider and the
> ftp progress bar (text mode in Terminal). I don't know if this is lack
> of double buffering or whatnot but this really brings the general Haiku
> perception down.

Well haiku is not double buffered at all (unless you use a video mode
with a depth other than 32 bit). By the way, in vesa mode, 16 bit
double buffered mode is much smoother (faster, even) than 32 bit mode.
At least on my system.
Moreover, our BSlider class is not double buffered, unlike R5 one (but
there's a define in the code which re-enables double buffering).
