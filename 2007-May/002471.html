Sorry for the late reply.  It took me a while to get through the background
tasks of life.  I agree with some of Pike's thoughts and disagree with others
(mixed case names), but in general it's admirable to see someone thinking
about what good programming is.

I can recommend "Code Complete, Second Edition" by Steve McConnell as a very
good going over of the things that go into making good code.  It's a big book,
but I found myself nodding my head many times.  And now I even understand why
people use the trailing opening parenthesis at the end of a line rather than on
a separate line - it has to do with a more rational way of thinking about
blocks of code.  Even though it's put out by Microsoft Press, it's not
operating system or language dependent (other than being about languages with
classes) and quite worth getting.

http://www.amazon.com/Code-Complete-Second-Steve-McConnell/dp/0735619670/
