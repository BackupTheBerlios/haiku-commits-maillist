I am no IP attorney, so don't take my opinion as legal council. However, 
seeing the Haiku devs pointing out this issue themselves makes me raise 
these questions.

The fact that the Haiku devs/admins themselves acknowledge potentially 
problematic areas such as a lack of compliance with all the licenses of 
the software included in the tests images distributed from the official 
Haiku Build Factory, and/or the inclusion of an mp3 decoder w/o 
permission is not very reassuring from a legal standpoint.

I agree with François here: the best would be to consult a lawyer, and 
this sounds like something that Haiku Inc. should be doing, hopefully 
sooner rather than later.

If you acknowledge a problem, the right think to do is to attempt to 
rectify it. I am hoping that Haiku does the right thing here.
