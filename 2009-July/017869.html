On 2009-07-06 at 15:43:05 [+0200], stippi at mail.berlios.de wrote:
> Log:
> Get the AVFormatReader into a usable state. DemuxerTable.cpp controls 
> which container formats we support. I hope I have turned on only those 
> that don't have an implementation already (did we support ASF already?). 
> I mostly tested with AVI and that works reasonably well, but I only 
> tested raw audio so far. However, the backend for Marcus' avi_reader is 
> much nicer than FFmpeg, so I think it should stay and have disabled the 
> support for AVI in AVFormatReader. A big disappointment is that MPG 
> containers only give scrambled video. It may be a problem of the 
> AVCodecDecoder, but I am not so sure. After all, it works fine for other 
> container formats.

To follow up on that one, after some tiring debugging sessions, it turns 
out that the FFmpeg libs are not being built correctly. Once I exchange the 
static libs to the ones I built with the build system of the FFmpeg 0.5 
release, all sorts of stuff starts working. MPEGs work and so do the clips 
from my AVCHD video camera (H.264 in MPEG-TS container). The most obvious 
difference is that I had to turn off all CPU acceleration in the FFmpeg 
build. For Haiku it's the other way around. There I can't turn it off if I 
want to get it building... don't know if that's the only difference.

At least now I know where to look for the actual problem... :-)
