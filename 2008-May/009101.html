Yup, it is broken. Hot-plugging a mass storage device apparently 
deadlocks the system and unplugging devices results in:

kernel>:strcmp
kernel>:get_device_for_path__FP13legacy_driverPCc + 0x0048
kernel>:republish_driver
kernel>:legacy_driver_rescan
kernel>:devfs_rescan_driver
.../usb>:RescanDrivers
.../usb>:ExploreThread

And my main PC does now not boot Haiku anymore because the hack 
employed to get the otherwise-standard ide controller to work (it 
advertises itself as 0x80 PCI_mass_storage_other instead of 0x01 
PCI_ide) doesn't seem to be enough anymore.

I'll see that I can get the USB into the new device manager soon. But I 
would prefer leaving the old APIs and also the current USB drivers as 
they are, as I still use most of them under R5.
