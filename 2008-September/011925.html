Fredrik Ekdahl wrote:
> Have you thought about having a libavformat based reader? I don't know 
> the details of the interface to the reader, but in my "simplified" view 
> of how it works, a libavformat reader would cover all our needs and a 
> whole lot more. Why have a lot of readers for one format each when there 
> is a single library that can read just about any format?
> 
> Disclaimer: My intention is not to belittle anyones work, just wondering.

To be honest, I think it would be great. I have not evaluated whether it 
may make even more sense to use (the demuxer part of) the VLC framework 
(libvlc) or libavformat "only". But to support a substantial amount of 
codecs is such a huge task, I think a wrapper would be nice. Look at how 
much work the FreeBSD compat layer has already saved us.

> >> How will the number of decoding threads be set? Based on number of 
> >> CPUs perhaps..
> > 
> > Single threaded AFAIK.  avcodec may have something for multiple threads 
> > but I didn't look into that.
> 
> They do. In the case of h.264 decoding, the only supported 
> multi-threading ATM is slice-based parallelism. Though there is an 
> experimental CABAC-based patch that is used successfully by ffdshow (for 
> windows). Using multiple threads is necessary for most dual-core CPUs for 
> decoding 1080p material, as one core simply isn't fast enough (except for 
> the fastest models).
> FFmpeg also supports multi-threaded mpeg-2 decoding, and encoding for a 
> couple of formats.

You may be confusing plain h.264 playback with Blue-Ray playback. Blue-Ray 
imposes huge performance requirements because streams have to be decrypted 
and encrypted all around the place. It's the reason why some graphics 
boards now come with audio units on-board, so that some de/encryption steps 
can be saved. IIRC, I could playback h.264 1080p just fine on my 1.83 GHz 
Core 2 Duo. I may be mixing something up though.
