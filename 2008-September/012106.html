> To me, it always felt that SetAutoResize() was added for Tracker specifically. It doesn't seem very flexible at least. The use case that I want to target is when you want to have explanatory text in your GUI. Or take BAlerts as an example. I am sure it is currently manually being figured out, how large the view needs to be to show the entire text. But what I want, is that the programmer can simply place a BTextView in the layout, and it will all be taken care of automatically, which means that the height of the control needs to adopt according to the width. This behaviour could also be specifically set on the control. SetAutoResize() could of course be used to mean exactly this in the layout management context. Maybe this is all you are saying... :-)

I wonder if they are different concepts.

AutoResize to me is saying that this control can adjust as the display
is adjusted.  The control grows in proportion to the display based on
weighting etc.

HeightForWidth is perhaps more about HOW the control resizes.  As the
width grows the height shrinks.
