> I don't like this change either, but it is definitely not the way to go 
> to just disable a whole interrupt vector. On my machines they are heavily 
> shared (like 5 devices). Disabling the whole vector makes all of them 
> unusable which is not a good thing. I have seen multiple bug reports now 
> that mention USB problems at least on the sidelines, which in fact are 
> not. But disabling the interrupt vector where the USB controller sits 
> obviously causes any operation to time out there.
> What would probably be best is to output warnings when these cases 
> happen, so one could still easily tell that there is a problem but 
> without interfering with other devices' function.
> As I noted, on my laptop it clearly is caused by HDA and I hope Axel will

> works easily enough, so it is not like all debugging is broken now.

I am also in favor Michael's change. Fixing kernel problems is not my area
