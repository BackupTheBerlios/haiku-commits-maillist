That's a good side effect, we at least have a choice now :-D
There are good arguments on both sides, i hesitated a lot, but for now i 
think that the best option is "copy by default", even if i think that 
"move by default" could be done right with some work (is it possible to 
have a trustable move?).
Maybe in the future we could have no default at all, by using other 
criterions per volume, as  "being on a different volume" isn't always a 
good reason to decide.

But anyway, whatever the OS thinks is best for you :), the crucial thing 
is the visual indication of what will happen when you release the 
button. So +1 for the mouse cursor overlay.

As for failing moves or other risky ops, in an ideal world, the computer 
would guess that you are doing something risky, and warn you friendly! 
"Hey user! Are you sure you want to _move_ this file to a floppy disk?" 
Dream or nightmare? :-P
Something that could be nice, in some cases, is a paranoid mode that 
does automatic data validation on copy or move (ex for remote volumes or 
writable cd/dvd).
