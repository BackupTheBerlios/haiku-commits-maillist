I tested now with 16 clipping rects, and there the performance did not 
degrade yet. (Test was changed to always run 5 seconds, btw.)

ZETA:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 16
Total lines rendered: 6000
Lines per second: 14027.869
Average time between iterations: 0.0153 seconds.

app_server test environment:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 16
Total lines rendered: 17700
Lines per second: 3640.921
Average time between iterations: 0.0002 seconds.

(26% of the ZETA performance, but fully anti-aliased).

When setting the pensize to 3, we begin to catch up:

ZETA:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 16
Total lines rendered: 6000
Lines per second: 4200.745
Average time between iterations: 0.0120 seconds.

app_server test environment:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 16
Total lines rendered: 8420
Lines per second: 1705.004
Average time between iterations: 0.0002 seconds.

(40% of the ZETA performance)

Without any clipping, we are even much faster with a pensize of 3:

ZETA:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 0
Total lines rendered: 6000
Lines per second: 4010.963
Average time between iterations: 0.0117 seconds.

app_server test environment:
Lines per iteration: 20
Clipping rects: 0
Total lines rendered: 25380
Lines per second: 5234.885
Average time between iterations: 0.0001 seconds.

(130% of ZETA performance)
