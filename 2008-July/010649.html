The random lines are actually quiet good. These are fully antialiased 
B_OP_OVER lines (B_OP_COPY is like B_OP_OVER for lines in Haiku). I 
consider four times slower quite good in this case. I used the app_server 
test environment on ZETA. Because the most drastic impact is when clipping 
is used, the rand() performance is probably not it. There is also pretty 
much no difference between single and random color, although the later has 
almost double the calls to rand().

That being said, 420 clipping rects are pretty unlikely. I guess usually it 
should be 3 to 5 on average. It only means that our code scales very badly 
to many more clipping rects.
