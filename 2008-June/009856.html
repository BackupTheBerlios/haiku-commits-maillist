> > rather
> > unlikely scenario, but something that might be interesting for certain
> > emulator setups.
> >
> 
> Pardon my ignorance, but what exactly do you mean by "initializing
> partitions with a partition map"?

A partition map (defining the primary partitions) normally resides directly 
on a the disk device. Haiku allows arbitrary nesting of partitioning systems, 
though. I.e. you could create a partition map in a partition. Mostly one 
wouldn't want to do that, but there are situations imaginable where one 
would. E.g. if you use an emulator in Haiku and assign a complete partition 
as disk for the guest OS, you could access the partitions of the guest OS 
from within Haiku.
