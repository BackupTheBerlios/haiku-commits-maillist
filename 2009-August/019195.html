> And I like how it's handled in Terminal and WonderBrush and ShowImage (but unfortunately
> not in MediaPlayer): have ALT +/- in/decrease zoom or size and one keycombo to set it back
> to default. Since we're with +/- on the right side of the keyboard, ALT+0 is most convenient.

Not at all for my azerty keyboard. I have + next to backspace but
needing shift to reach it, - on the 6 key and 0 needing shift to reach
it. Fortunately I can use the + and - in the numeric keypad, but these
need the fn key on my laptop. Putting symbol-based shortcuts on things
other than letters isn't a good idea, because on some keyboards you
need shift to reach the right symbol, and if using the same symbol
WITH shift for another shortcut, you can't reach one of them. This is
quite annoying. So avoid mixing symbolic shortcuts (+ and -) with
localized one (the key near + and -). At least until the locale kit
can change them :)
