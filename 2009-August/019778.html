> Btw, what's the bootloader on this ?

It's got a firmware called routerboot which I think is
vendor-specific. It will not likely be of any use with
other Haiku ports or even with other mips hardware.

IIRC it's very basic, letting you load an ELF image over
TFTP or from compact flash media formatted in a certain
way.

> Is it really mips specific ?

I believe the vendor is using its firmware with multiple
archs, but I don't expect any other Haiku port to target
their hardware. Headless is no fun in a Haiku context.
 
> You might want to use the board/ stuff as on ARM, and an
> arch/mipsel subdir here as well instead...

Yes, probably. It's still early. I'm mostly trying to 
get things to compile and link.

I'm sure people are wondering why I chose this hardware
and not some cool SGI box. My reasons are as simple as
this hardware is. It has a serial port. It's documented,
little-endian, is based on a chip implementing the more
recent 'MIPS32' ISA, is a SoC (has devices integrated),
and the firmware is simple. It hasn't got usb, ps2 or vga, 
so it will only ever be of interest to me, and to me only
as a stepping stone for Haiku/mips(el) onto other more
suitable hardware.

/Jonas.
