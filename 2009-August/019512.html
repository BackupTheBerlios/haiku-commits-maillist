> You mean like the Amiga menu bar flash?

I don't recall the menu bar flashing, but I do recall the window (or even 
the complete screen?) flashing when beeping. At least in the AmigaBASIC 
"IDE" and probably also in the terminal. Given how long ago I parted with 
my last Amiga and considering my advanced age, I might be mixing or making 
things up, though. :-)

> I suppose we don't have something like that. But if the hearing
> impaired concern you, we could have a (Deskbar?) notifier that flashes
> whenever something beeps :-)

I was not particularly thinking about hearing impaired people, but it 
concerns them, too of course. Maybe I'm the only one, but I just don't like 
the OS or applications to annoy me with noise, particularly when I'm 
listening to music. There might also be machines on which Haiku doesn't 
support the sound card.

I don't think a deskbar notifier would be helpful, since it would be 
unconnected to the application that causes the beep and the deskbar might 
not even be visible.

KDE's Konsole has "visual bell", but it's just a tiny icon at the bottom of 
the window which is too easily overlooked. I was rather thinking of 
flashing the complete window. Though that should be tested a bit, as it 
might be too annoying. Flashing the window's menu bar (or decor?) might be 
a good compromise.

CU, Ingo
