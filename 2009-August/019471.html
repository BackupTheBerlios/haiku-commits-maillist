"Axel Dörfler" <axeld at pinc-software.de> wrote:
> "Jonas Sundström" <jonas at kirilla.com> wrote:
> > IMO, any content that is part of a file (embedded)
> > should have authority over attributes. Attributes
> > are just a cache, IMO.
> 
> Ever came across people files? Attributes are more 
> than just a cache to the other file data.

I'm usually careful when I explain things and want 
people to understand what I'm saying, but it doesn't 
come natural for me to be super-careful when expressing
opinion*.

Saying that People files are stupid is an expression of
opinion and it does not by itself tell you much about 
what I think of attribute use in general. You'd have to
ask, or digest my other writing, or ask me to reformulate.

/Jonas.

*(I don't know if I'm irrevocably screwed up in the 
language department, but I find that the "IMO" at 
the end of that sentence makes for plenty of room to 
interpret it as something other that a robotic 
"Attributes - Are - A - Cache".

Like
"One can see attributes as cache of file content"

or
"Jonas likes to see attributes as a cache of file content".

I should probably have spelled it out into something
long and stating that this was my personal view of this:

"I think that attributes are often better used as a
cache for file content that you need fast access to
than as the primary and only means to store that data.")

/Jonas.
