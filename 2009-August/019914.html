On Tue, Aug 25, 2009 at 8:26 AM, Ingo Weinhold<ingo_weinhold at gmx.de> wrote:
>> You mean in an emulator? Because computers mostly don't come with serial
>> ports nowadays. Let elone someone having two computers in the first place
>> and both of them with serial ports even.
>
> Since we're mainly targetting developers and geeks, availability of a second
> computer is probably not a problem. Serial ports are indeed unfortunately
> rare.

I actually find them quite common still on ATX motherboards I buy,
they seem to be rare mostly on laptop computers and smaller form
factor machines these days.

>> I expect that if someone runs into some random bug, he won't have captured
>> the serial log. So he would need to prepare this anyways, and may as well
>> enable serial debugging specifically to capture more info on a certain bug.
>
> I'm thinking of the following situations:
>
> * When booting from a CD you can't change the settings anyway.
>
> * When something goes wrong during the boot, you also can't change the
> settings. There are boot menu settings to get the debug output on screen,
> but this can be rather unhandy and requires taking pictures.

Would be nice if the boot menu had a serial debug option ;)

> * When you run into some random KDL and want to capture the stack trace, you
> would have to take a picture instead of being able to grab the serial output.

Yes, there are times when I've attached the serial cable *after* a KDL
to get the info, so I agree.

>> For everyone else, active serial debugging seriously degrades the first
>> impression (like because of audio skipping, which we cannot afford at all
>> IMHO), which defeats a lot of the purpose of the alpha (to give a good
>> first impression).
>
> OK, I wasn't aware that the serial debugging was the cause of the audio
> skipping. I wonder why, since at least normally there should be very little
> output after booting.

Perhaps we had some extremely chatty audio drivers?

Also, serial debug does seem to increase boot times by a couple
seconds (my own experience)... not that this is terribly noticeable
for most people, but it can be as much as 15-20% of the boot time ;)

Perhaps we could just leave it enabled in the nightly builds (from
trunk) and steer people to those in the event that they have an issue
at boot?

- Urias
