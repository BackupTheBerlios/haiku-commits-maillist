Humdinger <humdingerb at googlemail.com> wrote:
 ...
> Maybe an add-on AddExtention - Alt+Opt+X would
> suffice. Invoked on a folder it would work 
> recursively on its contents and adds the MIME
> type's associated extension for every file.

It wouldn't help much in the Screenshot case.
Forgetting to add the extension manually or
forgetting to run AddExtension - same result.
 
> Maybe, this could be thought even further as a
> kind of Prepare-4-Export

I don't know. Seems like a lot of trying to
account for all possible recipients and transport
means for all filetypes and content. Even without
doing transcoding of media or converting between
fileformats (say Gobe to Word) it's just too much.

I wouldn't want to tell users to run such a tool
on everything they email, upload to facebook, etc.

>  that would also do 
> a Attributes2Tags where applicable.

IMO, any content that is part of a file (embedded)
should have authority over attributes. Attributes
are just a cache, IMO. Here I'm thinking primarily
about document and content type of files, like 
Word documents, jpegs and mp3s - things meant to be
shared - and not so much about local phenomenon such
as application settings, etc.

People files are stupid, IMO, since they're a type
of file you're likely to want to exchange with non-
BeOS/Haiku people and over non-BeOS/Haiku media
and transport. I love the attributes but I think
its madness not giving them any content that other
systems might understand.

/Jonas.
