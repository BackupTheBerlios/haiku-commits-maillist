Bryce Groff wrote:

>     It is not uncommon for devices like memory sticks and SD cards to not
>     have a partition at all and, instead, have the filesystem directly over
>     the raw device.
> 
> 
> I thought about this before I made the change. I came to the conclusion 
> that if a device was a raw filesystem the only thing you would be able 
> to do to it would be to initialize a new partitioning system which is 
> still available as an option. I am not sure what action would be taken 
> if you wanted to delete a raw filesystem on a device.

You can also mount it, of course. :)

Just as a reference, mounting a partition under linux would be something 
like:

mount /dev/sda1 /blah

In a device without partition and with the filesystem over the raw 
device, it would be:

mount /dev/sda /blah

Notice that the partition number indicator is omitted in this case.

You can not, of course, delete a partition in a device like this (it has 
no partition, after all) and creating a partition would most likely 
invalidate the filesystem even if the partition takes up the entire 
device. But you can mount it nevertheless.
