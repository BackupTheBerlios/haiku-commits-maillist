On Mon, 14 Jan 2008 15:11:32 +0100, Michael Lotz wrote
> > Shouldn't this be changed also in BView::SetFlags() ?
> 
> Not really. In SetFlags() you already provide a combined uint32 containing
> both resize mask and flags. SetFlags() only masks off invalid/unused 
> flags but then recombines them to the uint32 fFlags again.

Correction: In SetFlags() you only provide flags and in SetResizingMode() you
only provide the resizing mode. So there is now way you could accidentally mix
them up. In the constructor though it can easily happen if you write
BView(frame, name, flags, resizeMask) instead of the correct BView(frame,
name, resizeMask, flags).
