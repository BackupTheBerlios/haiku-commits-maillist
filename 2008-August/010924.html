That is entirely a political decision.
When OSS was closed, they started on ALSA, and let the version of OSS 
in the kernel tree get dusted and unmaintained.
When you hear ppl complain about OSS, it's about the years old version 
in the kernel tree, not OSSv4, which has most if not all of what ALSA 
peeps complain OSS lacked which makes them use ALSA.
Of course now they don't have much reasons to use OSS again (actually I 
heard of a distro that switched back to it), but OTH it's used on many 
other OSes.
