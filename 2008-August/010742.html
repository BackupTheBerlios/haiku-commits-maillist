Since there are more major changes to come in critical subsystems 
(particularly I/O and VM) which will almost inevitably be accompanied by 
some more bugs, maybe it's time to start tagging revisions that are 
generally perceived as relatively stable, and perhaps even maintain them 
for a short time as branches. That would be handy for people who want to 
demo Haiku or who want to get serious work done without running into every 
latest bug.
