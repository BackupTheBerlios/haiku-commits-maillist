> Sounds like a clean thing to do, but what could be the benefits of making
> this settable? After all, the human movement precision and the perception
> have limits. At first, I considered 1/60s, the usual screen refresh rate, to
> be a good number. Then I used double that just for kicks. Consider that the
> mouse device will buffer (pile up) intermediate movement measurements, so
> nothing is lost. In WonderBrush you have to move the mouse very fast and
> make enough of a turn to see edges in a stroke. Regular strokes, where you
> still have enough control and precision, should have enough samples per
> distance to look smooth. And if one wants more smoothness, it probably helps
> the user more to smooth out the mouse samples algorithmically (in
> WonderBrush), than by taking more samples per second. The only other
> interesting aspect would be games and some gamers believing their mouse
> needs to have 4000 dpi resolution. That resolution is of course not fully
> explored when taking too few samples per second, but I would not be easily
> convinced that 125 samples/s is not precise enough. Then again, I haven't
> stood beside a real gamer performing his art yet. ;-)
